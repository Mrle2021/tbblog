# Blog Stock Photo Style Guide

## Brand Direction

The blog discusses AI, society, philosophy, media literacy, history, and modern anxiety. Images should feel editorial, calm, intelligent, and restrained. Prefer symbolic stock photography over literal news imagery.

## Visual DNA

- Palette preference: indigo, warm orange, off-white, graphite, and muted teal when available, but natural photo quality is more important than exact colors.
- Style: quiet editorial photography, natural light, clean composition, minimal clutter, documentary but not sensational.
- Composition: generous negative space, strong central metaphor, clear foreground/background separation, and either centered balance or rule-of-thirds placement.
- Crop safety: photos must remain useful after 16:9 cover cropping and 4:3 figure cropping.
- Mood: rational, reflective, slightly futuristic, never sensational.

## Avoid

- Generic staged corporate handshakes, fake smiles, glossy 3D, cluttered dashboards, random floating text, readable text inside the image, watermarks, logos, UI screenshots, and close-up human faces.
- Literal news imagery that depends on real people, real brands, flags, or copyrighted characters.

## Search Query Guidance

Use short English search phrases. Good queries combine an abstract theme with a concrete visual metaphor:

- `quiet workspace notes laptop`
- `city reflection window thinking`
- `library archive documents`
- `person silhouette screen abstract`
- `newspaper table morning light`

Avoid overly specific Chinese titles as search terms; stock APIs work better with concise English concepts.
